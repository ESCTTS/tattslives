About us:

If you live in Redhill, Reigate, Merstham or any of the surrounding villages, such as Earlswood, Gatton or Salfords, then the Redhill History Centre in Redhill Library is for you. We have old maps, census returns, ancient documents, parish records, and history books galore for researching local and family history. Do you sometimes wonder where Grandad fought in the trenches and whether your Grannie marched with the Suffragettes? We can help you find out with online resources such as Ancestry and the British Newspaper Archive at our fingertips. We’re also good at providing support for local history students. Whether you’re in primary or secondary education or just interested in history we can help you find the sources you need. The Redhill History Centre based in Redhill Library has lots of answers, visit us - it’s free and you don’t need to book. We can help you find your story. Redhill History Centre is a partnership between Surrey Libraries, Surrey History Centre, Reigate and Redhill Local and Family History Group, Holmesdale Natural History Club and Reigate Priory Museum. We are all working together to bring you history.

How to contact Redhill History Centre

You will find the Redhill History Centre on the first floor of Redhill Library at the Harlequin Centre. You can visit and use the resources there for your own research any time the library is open. All our help is free as Redhill History Centre is part of the Surrey Library Service. You will also have free access to Ancestry, Find my Past and the British Newspaper Archives. Our helpful volunteer historians are always in the Redhill History Centre on the 1st and 3rd Saturday in the month from 2pm to 4pm. And by arrangement at other times. We also partner with the Holmesdale Natural History Club as well as the Reigate and District Family History Group and the Surrey History Centre, we are all working together to bring you history.
